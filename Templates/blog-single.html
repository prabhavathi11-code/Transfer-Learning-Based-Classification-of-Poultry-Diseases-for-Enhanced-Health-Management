Understanding Coccidiosis in Poultry

Published on June 30, 2025 | By PoultryDetect Team

Coccidiosis is a parasitic disease of the intestinal tract of poultry caused by coccidian protozoa. It is one of the most economically significant diseases affecting poultry worldwide.

Chickens become infected when they ingest sporulated oocysts from contaminated food, water, or litter. The parasites multiply in the intestinal lining, causing damage that can lead to bleeding, poor nutrient absorption, and death.

Prevention: Good hygiene, proper ventilation, dry litter, and vaccination are key. Avoid overcrowding and maintain a clean environment to reduce the chances of spreading the disease.

Symptoms: Bloody droppings, weight loss, decreased egg production, ruffled feathers, and lethargy.
